Date: October 21st to November 11th 2022

Title: Kerala - the sweetest karela

In the beautiful land of Kerala, bitter gourd, or "Karela," tells a tale of culinary magic and flavors. Kerala's skilled chefs transform Karela into delectable dishes like Pavakka Thoran, Pavakka Mezhukkupuratti, and the famous Pavakka Pachadi. Kerala's love for Karela extends to seafood delicacies like Karimeen Pollichathu, where the bitter gourd adds a unique twist. From Karela Kichadi to Karela Kalan, Kerala showcases the versatility and adaptability of this humble vegetable. Discover the sweetness within Kerala's Karela-infused culinary wonders and embark on a flavor-filled journey through this enchanting land.

Date: September 21st to October 11th 2021

Title: Hyderabad

Welcome to the enchanting city of Hyderabad, where the aroma of aromatic spices and the sizzle of flavorful delicacies fill the air. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey like no other. Hyderabad is synonymous with its iconic dish, the world-famous "Hyderabadi Biryani." Fragrant basmati rice, tender meat, and a perfect blend of spices come together to create a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving more. Hyderabad, with its rich culinary traditions and diverse flavors, offers a gastronomic delight like no other. Immerse yourself in the vibrant tapestry of tastes and indulge in the mouthwatering delicacies that make this city a food lover's paradise.

Date: August 21st to September 11th 2020

Title: Mumbai

Mumbai's street food scene is legendary, and the iconic "Vada Pav" takes center stage. Mumbai is known for its coastal cuisine, and "Bombay Duck Fry" is a must-try delicacy. Despite its name, it is not duck but a flavorful fish marinated in spices and deep-fried to perfection. Don't miss out on the famous "Mumbai-style Cutting Chai." Mumbai, with its diverse culinary landscape, offers a sensory journey through the flavors of street food, coastal delights, and indulgent treats. Immerse yourself in the vibrant food culture of this city and experience the melting pot of flavors that make Mumbai a true gastronomic paradise.

Date: January 21st to February 11th 2019

Title: Shimla

Start your gastronomic journey with "Madra," a traditional Himachali dish made with soaked and boiled chickpeas in a yogurt-based gravy. For meat lovers, "Tudkiya Bhat" is a must-try. This aromatic rice dish is cooked with chunks of meat, a variety of spices, and the unique addition of "Pahari" (local) rajma, giving it a distinct and satisfying taste. Shimla, with its charming vistas and culinary treasures, offers a tantalizing experience for food enthusiasts. From hearty traditional dishes to local specialties, Shimla's cuisine invites you to explore the flavors of the Himalayas and create lasting culinary memories.

Date: March 21st to April 11th 2018

Title: Leh & Ladakh

Experience the traditional Ladakhi dish, "Thukpa," a hearty noodle soup that warms both body and soul. Made with homemade noodles, vegetables, and tender chunks of meat, Thukpa is a comforting delicacy and a staple of Ladakhi cuisine. Indulge in "Momos," the famous Himalayan dumplings that have gained popularity worldwide. These steamed or fried dumplings, filled with a variety of savory fillings like meat, vegetables, or cheese, offer a delightful burst of flavors with every bite. Leh and Ladakh offer more than just breathtaking landscapes; they present a culinary expedition that showcases the flavors of the region. From nourishing soups and delightful dumplings to unique bread and indigenous jams, the gastronomic delights of Leh and Ladakh will leave you with a taste of the Himalayas to cherish forever.

Title: Royal Rajasthan

Indulge in the iconic "Dal Baati Churma," a quintessential Rajasthani dish that combines lentils (dal) with baked wheat bread (baati) and a sweet crumbled mixture of wheat, ghee, and sugar (churma). This hearty and flavorful combination is a true delight. Savor the royal flavors of "Laal Maas," a spicy meat curry made with tender pieces of mutton or lamb cooked in a rich gravy of red chili paste, yogurt, and a blend of aromatic spices. It is a signature dish that exemplifies the fiery Rajasthani cuisine. Rajasthan, with its grandeur and rich cultural heritage, offers a culinary journey fit for royalty. From soul-satisfying dal baati churma to spicy laal maas and the array of flavors in a traditional thali, Rajasthan's cuisine is a celebration of opulence and taste. Immerse yourself in the regal feast of flavors and create lasting memories of Rajasthan's gastronomic splendors.
